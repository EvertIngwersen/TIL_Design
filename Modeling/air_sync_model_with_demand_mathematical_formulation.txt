Mathenatical formulation:

We present a multi-objective mixed integer programming model of the timetabling problem for all trains I that make use of the target railroad line. There
are two kinds of trains running on the line. Thee first kind of train I^T serves the transfer station. In other words, these trains
stop at the transfer station. Thee second kind of trains I^N does not serve the transfer station. e transfer synchronization
between trains and ights can be improved by adjusting the
timetable of the first kind of trains. Meanwhile, due to the
interaction between trains, the timetables of other trains might
have to be adjusted, too, to guarantee safety constraints. The
timetable is adjusted within a planning horizon, and we let H
be the upper bound of the time horizon.

Objective function:

Maximizing the Number of Synchronizations. For train-
ight connections, a synchronization in an AH integration
service is valid if the diference between the arrival of a
train i and the departure of a flight k is within a separation
time window  [\underline{b}, \overline{b}] at the transfer station As we assume the flight timetable
to be given, the departure time of any ight is fixed. Thuus, a
synchronization between a train i and a flight k is valid if the
arrival time of the train is within the connection time window
of the flight k at the transfer node. The departure time D_{\underline{k}} of the outgoing flight k minus the transfer time \upperline{b} is the lower bound of the 
conncection time window l_k and the departure time D_k minus the transfer time \underline{b} is the upper bound of the connection time window u_K:

1): l_k = D_k - \upperline{b}
2): u_k = D_k - \underline{b}

Therefore, the firrst objective function
– denoted by Z_1
 – is put forward to maximize the number of
synchronizations. Also in this objective function, the demand is also modelled:

3): Max Z_1 = sum_{i in I_T, k in K_out} D^{out}_k \cdot P_{i,k} + sum_{i in I_T, m in K_in} D^{in}_m \cdot Q_{i,m}

Maximizing coverage of synchronized flights: The second objective Z_2, aiming to maximize
the coverage of the ights by the trains, is proposed. In this constraint, the demand is also modelled:

    4): Max Z_2 = sum_{k in K_out} D^{out}_k \cdot C_k + sum_{m in K_in} D^{in}_m \cdot C_in_m

Minimizing the Transfer Penalties of Passengers. The different preferences of passengers should also be considered. Even
though a train i synchronizes with a flight k, passengers on the
train i perceive transfer times differently. Here we introduce a continuous non negative penalty variable p_{i,k} reflecting the specific preferences of business and Leisure passangers from the trai i to the flight k for the transfer time. The third
objective Z_3 (with much lower priority than the above two
objectives) is to minimize these transfer penalties:

5): Min Z_3 = sum_{i in I_T, k in K_out} p_{i,k} + sum_{i in I_T, m in K_in} p_in_{i,m}

Constraints:

6): a_{i,s+1} - d_{i,s} \geq r_{i,s} + \beta_{i,s}x_{i,s} + \gamma_{i,s+1}x_{i,s+1} \forall i \in I, s \in S_i\{des_i}

7): a_{i,s+1} - d_{i,s} \leq r_{i,s} + \beta_{i,s}x_{i,s} + \gamma_{i,s+1}x_{i,s+1} + y_{i,s} \forall i \in I, s \in S_i\{des_i}

3.3.3. Safety Headway Constraints. Limits on the headways for
all trains i \in I should be satisfied to ensure operational safety.
Constraints (16) and (17) ensure that the difference between
the departures of any two trains at station s (visited by the both
trains) satisfies a lower bound HD_s. Analogously, constraints
(18) and (19) ensure that the difference between the arrivals
of any two trains at station s satises a lower bound HA_s. M is
a large number that is no smaller than H + max (HD_s,HA_s):

16): d_{j,s} - d_{i,s} + M(1-O_{i,j}^s) \geq HD_s
17): d_{i,s} - d_{j,s} + M*O_{i,j}^s \geq HD_s \forall i,j \in I, s = xs_{i,j} \cdots xe_{i,j}-1
18): a_{j,s+1} - a_{i,s+1} + M(1-O_{i,j}^s) \geq HA_{s+1}

3.3.5. Synchronization Number Constraints. Constraint (21)
is the synchronization number constraint between trains and
ights at the transfer station.  the constraint states that if the
arrival time of a train i is within the connection time window [l_k, u_k] generated by a flight k which means
that the train can synchronize with the outgoing fligh k then the value of the variable P_{i,k}^s =1, 0 otherwise:

21): P_{i,k} = 1 if l_k \leq a_{i,s} \leq u_{k}, 0 otherwise \forall i \in I^T, k \in K, s=st. 

Constraint (21) can be linearized into constraints (22) and
(23) using a large positive value M'. As can be observed in
constraints (22) and (23), if a_{i,s} < l_k or a_{i,s} > u_k, the P_{i,k} = 0. 
If l_k \leq a_{i,s} u_k the  in principle P_{i,k} could either be 0 or 1, but the maximization objective function will force it to have a value
of one. M' is a large number and its value is greater than or equal to H

22): a_{i,s} \geq l_k - M'(1-P_{i,k}) \forall i \in I^T, k \in K, s=st.
23): a_{i,s} \leq u_k + M'(1-{_{i,k}) \forall i \in I^T, k \in K, s=st.

3.3.6. Coverage Constraints. The coverage variable C_k
represents whether the flight k is synchronized with at least
one feeder train. Because of the maximization objective
function Z_2, the variable P_{i,k} must only be set to one, if some
of the corresponding variables P_{i,k} have already been set to
one. Therefore,constraint 24 must be satisfied for any flight:

24): C_k \leq \sum_{i \in I^T} P_{i,k} k \in K

3.3.7. Passenger Preference Constraints. As mentioned above,
intermodal passengers have different preferences for transfer
times. We divide passengers into two types based on the purpose
of trips: business and leisure passengers. Business passengers
prefer shorter transfer times while leisure passengers prefer
longer transit times to reduce the risk of missing the plane. For a valid synchronization P_{i,k} between a train i and a flight
k, business passengers from the train i to the flight k are less
sensitive to the short transfer time and could thus prefer arriving
at the transfer station during [l_k, (l_k+u_k)/2]; when the transfer time exceeds (l_k+u_k)/they become sensative and the penalty should increase for example 
from (l_k+u_k)/2 to u_k. In contrast, leisure
passengers prefer slightly longer transfer times and they would
like to arrive at the transfer station during [l_k, (l_k+u_k)/2]. They
are more sensitive to short transfer times, and thus the penalty
should increase there. Theerefore, we introduce a continuous non-negative penalty variable p_{i,k}
representing the preferences of the passengers from the train
i to the flight k for the transfer time, as shown in constraints (25)–(27). Due to the 
objective function Z_3 the optimization model will push p_{i,k} to the smallest possible value. 
M'' is a large positive number with a value no less than H × max (v1 × w1, v2 × w2):

25): p_{i,k} \geq v_1 \times w_1 \times (a_{i,s} - \frac{l_k+u_k}{2}) - (1-P_{i,k})\times M'' \forall i \in I^t, k \in K, s=st.
26): p_{i,k} \geq v_2 \times w_2 \times (\frac{l_k+u_k}{2} - a_{i,s}) - (1-P_{i,k})\times M'' \forall i \in I^t, k \in K, s=st.
27): p_{i,k} \geq 0 \forall i \in I^T, k \in K

Priori Method. We choose to apply the a priori method to our multi-objective model. In our formulation, the
first and second objectives have much higher priority than
does the third objective. The first two objectives aim to determine the synchronization events. Subsequently, the third-level
optimization is to further narrow the solutions by minimizing the transfer penalties of different passengers. We aim to
provide more smooth transfers, and thus, the rst objective
has a higher priority than does maximizing the coverage of
synchronized ights. The multi-objective model is converted
into three single-objective problems that can be solved exactly
by any optimization solver

First, we propose a single objective model for maximizing Z_1, denoted by M1

Model M1:
Objective function (3).
Subject to constraints (6)–(20) and (22)–(23).
Output: P (the maximum number of synchronizations).

The maximum number of synchronizations is fixed and we
impose an addition constraint (28).

28): \sum_{i \in I^T} \sum_{k \in K} P_{i,k} = P 

Then, the second single objective model for maximizing Z_2 denoted by M2 is proposed:

Model M2:
Objective function (4).
Subject to constraints (6)–(20), (22)–(24) and (28).
Output: C (the maximum number of synchronized ights
while maximizing the number of synchronizations).

The maximum coverage of synchronized ights is fixed, and
we impose another constraint (29):

29): \sum_{k \in K} C_k = C

The last single-objective model M3 is then reformulated as
follows to minimize the transfer penalties of passengers. We
can obtain the adjusted timetable.
Model M3:
Objective function (5).
